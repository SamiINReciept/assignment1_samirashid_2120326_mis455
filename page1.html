Human-Computer Interaction
From immersive needfinding techniques to robust user evaluation methods and insightful data analysis approaches, the course (CSE490-HCI) has equipped me with a diverse toolkit for crafting intuitive interfaces. Moreover, our hands-on project in HCI has been a transformative experience. Focused on addressing stress management challenges among healthcare providers in Bangladesh, this project not only underscores the practical relevance of HCI principles but also serves as the cornerstone of my undergraduate thesis. As we continue to refine and develop this initiative, I'm excited to contribute meaningfully to both academia and real-world problem-solving.
The Design Science Research (DSR) - is a methodology in information systems and computer science. It aims to solve complex problems through systematic design, implementation, and evaluation of innovative artifacts like software systems or methodologies. This iterative approach emphasizes both scientific rigor and practical relevance, making it valuable for addressing real-world challenges efficiently.
Quantitative data analysis: Employing statistical techniques to examine numerical data, offering insights into patterns and relationships with precision.
Qualitative data analysis: Interpreting non-numerical data to discern themes and meanings, providing nuanced insights into diverse perspectives and complex phenomena.
